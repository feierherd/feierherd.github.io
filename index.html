Thank you for visiting my website. I am an assistant professor at Universidad de San Andrés in Buenos Aires, Argentina.

I conduct theoretical and empirical research on political economy, with a focus on processes of accountability in developing democracies. I am also interested on the political causes and consequences of economic inequality.

I received my PhD in Political Science (with Departmental and University Distinction) from Yale University in 2017. Before returning to Argentina, I was also a Postdoctoral Associate at Duke University.

I am currently a research associate for the Center for the Politics of Development at UC Berkeley and the Center for the Study of Human Development at San Andrés.
